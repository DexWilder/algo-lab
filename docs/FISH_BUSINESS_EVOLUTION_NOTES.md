### Tier 2: Knowledge Products (Medium complexity)

**Educational Content**
- Document the FQL methodology for others
- Potential formats: blog, course, YouTube, newsletter
- Based on: operating principles, pipeline design, validation approach
- Risk: exposes methodology. Mitigate by teaching principles, not parameters.
- Revenue: subscription or course fees

**Consulting / Advisory**
- Help other systematic traders build their infrastructure
- Based on FQL's architecture patterns (not proprietary strategies)
- Risk: time commitment competes with system operation
- Revenue: hourly or project-based

### Tier 3: Capital Structures (High complexity)

**Family Office Structure**
- Formal entity managing Fisher family capital
- Advantages: tax optimization, estate planning, separation of personal/trading assets
- Requirements: legal counsel, accounting, entity formation
- Timing: when AUM exceeds ~$500K and annual returns justify the overhead

**Fund Structure**
- Manage outside capital alongside family capital
- Requirements: legal structure (LP/LLC), compliance, investor relations
- Advantages: management fees + performance fees, larger capital base
- Risks: regulatory burden, investor pressure, career risk
- Timing: only after 2+ years of auditable live track record

**Membership / Signal Service**
- Provide trade signals or portfolio allocations to subscribers
- Lower regulatory burden than a fund
- Risk: reputation risk if performance declines, IP exposure
- Timing: only after the system is proven and automated

## Entity and Tax Considerations (Consult Professional)

These are notes for future discussion with a tax attorney/CPA:

- **Trader Tax Status (TTS):** If trading is your primary activity, may qualify for TTS which allows mark-to-market accounting and business expense deductions
- **LLC vs S-Corp:** Trading through an entity may provide liability protection and tax advantages
- **Section 1256 Contracts:** Futures are 60/40 long-term/short-term capital gains regardless of hold period — significant tax advantage
- **Retirement Accounts:** Some prop firm structures may allow retirement account funding
- **State Considerations:** Florida, Texas, Wyoming, Nevada have no state income tax

**Action:** Consult a tax professional specializing in active traders before making entity decisions. Do not structure based on internet advice.

## Structural Advantages to Preserve

As FISH evolves, these advantages must be protected:

1. **Small capital advantage** — can trade edges that break at scale
2. **No investor pressure** — optimize for Sharpe, not AUM growth
3. **Full automation** — the system runs without daily discretion
4. **Speed of iteration** — can test and deploy ideas in hours, not months
5. **Privacy** — no public track record requirement unless chosen
6. **Generational horizon** — can accept short-term volatility for long-term compounding

Any business evolution that compromises these advantages should be rejected
or carefully designed to preserve them.

## Decision Framework

Before pursuing any business evolution option, answer:

1. **Does this require forward evidence I don't have yet?** If yes, wait.
2. **Does this compromise the system's core advantages?** If yes, redesign or reject.
3. **Does this require more time than operating the system?** If yes, delay until the system is more autonomous.
4. **Is this reversible?** Prefer reversible decisions (blog, dashboard) over irreversible ones (fund launch, entity formation).
5. **Does this serve the family mission?** If it only serves ego or short-term income, reconsider.
