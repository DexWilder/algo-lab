## Why It Failed

### 1. Sweep detection is too noisy on 5m data
The strategy requires detecting wicks beyond the opening range that close back inside. On 5m bars, minor volatility spikes constantly trigger false sweeps — especially on MES/MNQ where intraday range expansion is frequent.

### 2. Pullback entry is too loose
The pullback confirmation (close below prior bar low → next bar close above prior bar high) has no quality filter. It fires on any 2-bar reversal pattern, regardless of trend context, volume, or momentum. This generates entries into chop.

### 3. Fixed stop distances don't scale with volatility
Using fixed 5pt stops on MES and 25pt on MNQ ignores daily volatility variance. On high-volatility days the stops are too tight; on low-volatility days the R:R is poor. ATR-adaptive stops would be more appropriate.

### 4. 1 trade/day cap limits recovery
After a losing sweep entry, the strategy is locked out for the rest of the day. Combined with a 30% win rate, this means most days produce a single loss with no opportunity to recover.

### 5. Sweep bias itself may not be predictive on these assets
The ICT framework assumes institutional order flow creates sweeps with directional intent. On micro futures (MES/MNQ/MGC), the liquidity dynamics differ from full-size contracts — retail participation is higher and sweeps may be noise rather than signal.

## Salvageable Components

### State machine architecture
The WAITING → RANGE_FORMED → SWEEP_DETECTED → PULLBACK_ENTRY state machine pattern is clean and reusable. Could be adapted for other session-based strategies that need multi-phase logic within a trading day.

### Session range calculation
The opening range (first 30 min high/low) calculation and daily reset logic is solid and already used by ORB strategies. No unique value over existing ORB components.

### Sweep detection logic
The wick-beyond-close-inside pattern could be useful as a **filter** rather than a primary entry signal. For example: "only take breakout entries if a sweep occurred first" — this inverts the logic from "sweep = reversal" to "sweep = confirmation."

**Recommendation:** Extract the state machine pattern to `research/strategy_components/` for reuse. The sweep detection could become a filter component (`FILTER-SWEEP-CONFIRM-001`) that enhances other strategies.

## Lessons Learned

1. **ICT concepts need adaptation for micro futures** — the framework was designed for full-size ES/NQ with institutional flow. Micro contracts have different market microstructure.
2. **Fixed stops are dangerous** — always prefer ATR-adaptive or structure-based stops.
3. **1 trade/day strategies need high win rates** — with no intraday recovery mechanism, WR < 50% is fatal.
4. **Faithful conversion can confirm a strategy has no edge** — this is a valuable research outcome, not a waste. Now we know this logic doesn't work and can focus elsewhere.
